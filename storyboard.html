Screen Recording Shots
01 App main window — the floating keyboard pill UI overlay on desktop. Show it appearing above a Gmail compose window.
02 AI generation flow — user types 4 words, SnapText expands to a full paragraph. Record at 60fps, slow to 40% in edit.
03 Context switching — cut between Gmail, Slack, LinkedIn reply box. Show the same pill appearing each time.
04 Onboarding/settings — brief 1s flash of a clean settings panel showing "personalize your tone" options.
05 Keyboard heatmap — if possible, a stylized animation showing SnapText reducing keystrokes by 70%.
Music Direction
BPM 90–110 BPM — tight enough to feel purposeful, slow enough to not rush the viewer.
FEEL Ambient electronic + minimal techno — think Tycho, Jon Hopkins, or early Four Tet. Clean, forward-moving.
HOOK Music builds in Scene 3 (demo), peaks during the metric reveal in Scene 4, then strips back to a single tone for Scene 5.
SFX Subtle key click SFX in Scene 1. Soft "ping" when SnapText generates a response. Logo whoosh in Scene 2.
SOURCE Suggest: Artlist, Musicbed, or Epidemic Sound — search "ambient productivity tech loop."
LinkedIn Post Copy
L1 Hook line: "I tracked my typing for a week. I typed the same sentences 47 times. So I built a fix."
L2 Body: Introduce SnapText. 2–3 sentences on the problem + what it does. Keep it personal and founder-voiced.
L3 Proof: "Users write 4.7× faster. It learns your tone. It works in every app you already use."
CTA Close: "Link in comments. Try it free." (LinkedIn suppresses links in post body — put the URL in first comment.)

// Motion Style Guide
Easing & Timing
Animation Principles
Entrance cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1) — spring feel, overshoots slightly
Exit ease-in · fast exit, 200ms max
Transitions Cross-dissolve 600ms or hard-cut — no wipes, no spins
Text reveal Staggered lines, 80ms delay between words, fade + translateY(8px)
Numbers Count-up with ease-out · 1.2s total duration
Rule Nothing rotates. No bounce. Gravity is respected.
